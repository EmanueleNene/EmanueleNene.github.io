Material Science

Cellulose Nanofibers

Investigation of mechanical properties of CNF-reinforced composites for structural applications.

Overview

Cellulose Nanofibers (CNF) represent a paradigm shift in sustainable materials. This research investigates how these high-strength fibers can be used to reinforce composites, rivaling the strength-to-weight ratio of traditional engineering materials.

Methodology

Our approach involves advanced microscopic imaging and mechanical stress testing to understand the bonding mechanisms between fibers at a nanoscopic level.

Future Prospects

Applications range from lightweight automotive parts to bio-mechanical implants, leveraging the biocompatibility and incredible strength of plant-derived nanofibers.
