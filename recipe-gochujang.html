Gochujang Glazed Chicken

A deeply flavored Korean-inspired chicken glaze, balancing sweet, spicy, and umami.

What is Gochujang?

Gochujang (고추장) is a fermented Korean red chili paste made from glutinous rice, fermented soybeans, red chili powder, and salt. Its flavor profile is simultaneously spicy, sweet, umami, and slightly sour due to fermentation — quite different from fresh chili heat. The Scoville range is typically 1,000–10,000 SHU depending on the brand, but the fermented depth is what defines it.

Ingredients

Serves: 2–3 | Time: ~35 min

Marinade/Sauce

  • 2 tbsp Gochujang paste
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp Honey or brown sugar
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp Neutral oil (for cooking)

Protein & Garnish

  • 500g Chicken thighs (bone-in or boneless — thighs hold moisture far better than breast under high-heat glazing)
  • Garnish: Sesame seeds, thin-sliced spring onion, lime wedge

Method

1. Marinate

Mix all sauce ingredients. Score the chicken skin/flesh lightly (3–4 shallow cuts) — this accelerates marinade penetration and prevents skin from curling. Coat well and marinate at least 30 min, ideally 2–4 hours refrigerated.

2. Cook

Pan method: Heat a cast iron or stainless skillet over medium-high. Add oil. Sear chicken skin-side down 6–7 min without moving — you want a caramelized crust (the sugars in gochujang and honey will char if heat is too high, so medium-high is intentional). Flip, reduce heat to medium, cook another 8–10 min to internal temp 74°C / 165°F.

Oven method: 200°C / 400°F, 25–30 min, baste once at halfway.

3. Rest & Finish

Rest: Let rest 5 min before cutting — this redistributes juices back into the fibers.

Finish: Optionally reduce any leftover marinade in the pan with a splash of water to make a glossy sauce to spoon over.

Serving & Adjustments

Serve with: Steamed jasmine or short-grain rice, quick-pickled cucumber (rice vinegar + salt + sugar, 15 min), and a fried egg if you want a proper bibimbap-adjacent bowl.

Key Ratios to Remember

The sauce balance relies on five key elements. Adjusting any one shifts the whole profile:

  • Fat: Sesame oil
  • Acid: Vinegar
  • Heat & Umami: Gochujang
  • Sweet: Honey
  • Salt: Soy sauce

Pro Tip: If it's too spicy, increase honey. If it tastes flat, add more vinegar or soy.
