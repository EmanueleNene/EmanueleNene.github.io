Chicken Adobo

A masterclass in the Filipino braising technique: salty, acidic, and perfectly glazed.

The Technique

Inspired by the Philippines, this technique is a braise: meat is partially submerged in a salty–acidic liquid, gently simmered, then the liquid is reduced into a glossy glaze.

1. Core Ratio (The Foundation)

Soy : Vinegar : Water = 1 : 1 : 1.5

Ingredients for 1.2–1.5 kg chicken

(About 8–10 wings + 4–6 drumsticks)

  • 120 ml Soy sauce (½ cup)
  • 120 ml Vinegar (½ cup) - Rice vinegar for milder, white for sharper
  • 180 ml Water (¾ cup)
  • 6–8 cloves Garlic, crushed
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1 tsp Whole black peppercorns
  • Initial liquid total: ~420 ml
  • Final sauce: ~200–280 ml (typically 2–4 tbsp per portion)

*Optional: 1–2 tbsp brown sugar or honey to balance acidity.

2. Step-by-Step Logic

Step A — Optional Browning

Sear chicken skin-side down in a lightly oiled pot until golden. This triggers the Maillard reaction, adding depth and complexity. Remove excess fat if needed.

Step B — Building the Braise

  • Add garlic and sauté briefly (do not burn).
  • Add soy, vinegar, water, bay, and pepper.
  • Critical: Do not stir immediately after adding vinegar. Traditional reasoning suggests that allowing vinegar to boil undisturbed reduces harsh raw acidity.
  • Bring to a gentle boil.

Step C — The Simmer (Tenderization)

Lower heat and cover. The acid helps denature proteins and tenderize tissue.

  • Wings: ~20–25 min
  • Drumsticks: ~30–35 min

Step D — The Reduction (Concentration)

Remove the lid and simmer uncovered for 10–15 minutes. Liquid reduces by evaporation, concentrating saltiness and acidity. The sauce thickens from chicken-released gelatin. Aim for a glossy sauce at ~1/3–1/2 the original volume.

3. Texture Control Options

Crisp Skin

Remove chicken after braising. Reduce sauce separately. Broil or air-fry chicken for 5–8 mins, then spoon glaze over.

Soft & Saucy

Leave chicken in the pot and reduce the sauce around it for a more traditional, tender result.

4. Flavor Adjustments

Goal Adjustment
More savory depth Add 1 tsp fish sauce
Slight sweetness 1 tbsp brown sugar
Earthy tone Add ½ sliced onion
Spicy kick 1–2 dried chilies
Richer body ½ tsp cornstarch slurry at end

5. Why It Works (Technical View)

Adobo is a sophisticated balance of Salt (seasoning), Acid (stability & brightness), and Heat+Time (collagen to gelatin conversion). Reduction koncentrates these flavors into a powerful glaze.

Pro Tip: It tastes even better the next day as diffusion continues and the salt penetrates deeper into the meat.

6. Storage & Meal Prep

  • Refrigeration: Keeps well for 4–5 days.
  • Freezing: Freezes exceptionally well.
  • Reheating: Gently with 1–2 tbsp of added water to loosen the sauce.
